\chapter*{Lay Abstract}

%A lay abstract of not more 150 words must be included explaining the key goals and contributions of the thesis in lay terms that is accessible to the general public. This page must be numbered ‘iii’.
This thesis investigates the definition of a Model-Driven Engineering approach to managing traceability variability between product families and requirements. The approach manages traceability from the concept phase — that is, a product idea — and produces traceable elements as a side-effect of Model-Driven Engineering principles and practices. It shows how to connect goals, use cases, requirements and feature models to attempt to enable end-to-end traceability for product line engineering.
